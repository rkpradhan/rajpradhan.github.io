About Me

Hello!!!
I am Raj Kumar Pradhan. I am a teacher of physics at Amrit Campus, Tribhuvan University (TU), KTM, working there from Feb 2020 as a temporary faculty. I received a master's degree in Physics from Tribhuvan University in 2018. I have done my master dissertation entitled "The Age and Kinematic Distributions of the Milky Way Stellar Halo". During my master's degree, I worked with Dr. Prajwal Kafle and Professor Ajay K Jha in Galactic Dynamics. After getting an MSc degree, I visited IUCAA, Pune, India and then the University of Southampton, UK in 2019 for attending the SITARE Workshop program. I received a B.Sc. degree from Tri-Chandra College, Nepal in 2013 in Physics. I am also a visiting faculty of Nepal College of Information Technology where I supervise the lab-projects and give an assignment to the students. In addition, I am a research coordinator at Pokhara Astronomical Society and have been working there since 2017.

Research

My research interests concentrate on Galactic Archeology, the distribution and nature of dark matter and the stellar streams. Using Gaia, Galah and other surveys I am looking for dynamics of the stellar halo, disk, and the stellar stream such as Sagittarius. Currently, I am working on the following subtopics;

  • Stellar Streams
  • Stellar Halo
  • Stellar Disk
  • Programming

    • Current Research Projects

    1. Anisotropy of the Milky Way stellar halo using Gaia and GALAH:
    In this project, we will study the kinematics properties of the milky way stellar halo within 15 kpc. The stellar halo encodes the most striking feature of the formation history of the Milky Way galaxy and its evolution. We will use data from Gaia DR2 and GALAH to estimate the velocity dispersion profiles of BHB and K-giants halo stars. In this project, we will use modern statistical techniques, Bayesian inference to reproduce Kafle et al. 2017 (including me), S Bird et al. 2018 and Sharma et al. 2020.

    2 . The Kinematic properties of the Sagittarius stream using Gaia DR2 Kgaints, RR Lyrae, and M- Giants.
    The Sagittarius stream is one of the best tools to estimate the mass and shape of our Galaxy. So we are looking for its kinematic features and properties to understand the shape of our Galaxy. In this project, we use some tracers such as K-Giants and RR Lyrae and M-Giants stars with data from Gaia DR2. We will use mainly the papers, Antoja et al. 2020 and LW10 ... to get our results.

    3. Rotation curve of the Milky Way from classical Cepheids and RR Lyrae using Gaia DR2
    We will study the rotation curve of the MIlky Way using Gaia DR from classical Cepheids variable and RR Lyrae stars. We will use modern statistical tools to infer the result of circular velocity of the stars. Recently, P. Mroz et al 2018, Ablimit et al 2020 estimate the rotational velocity of classical Cepheids variable stars using different survyes.
